What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to understand, use, and manage one's emotions. It is also the ability to recognise and relate emotions to the people around you.

Researchers, John Mayer and Peter Salovey, first conceived the term in 1990 and later it was popularised by Daniel Goleman (a psychologist).

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ IQ
Stands for Emotional Quotient. Intelligent Quotient.
Measures Emotional Competency. Cognitive Abilities.
Abilities Identifying, evaluating, controlling, and expressing one's own emotions. Perceiving and assessing other's emotions. Logical reasoning, cognitive functions, memory, word comprehension, maths skills, abstract and spatial thinking, mental agility.
Affects Motivation, empathy, relationships, self-awareness, self-control. Academics, expertise, critical thinking, logic.
How are they Measured? Mayer-Salovey-Caruso Emotional Intelligence Test; Emotional and Social Competence Inventory. Stanford-Binet Intelligence Scale; Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale; Wechsler Intelligence Scale for Children; Woodcock-Johnson Tests of Cognitive Abilities.

It's important to note that EQ and IQ are not inherit, unchangeable qualities, and that a person's environment that affect on's EQ and IQ. The measurements of EQ and IQ can also be affected by bias hence leading to limitations in these tests.

EQ test results are not an accurate predictor of academic and work success because the test results are subjective. Often times, people want to do well on the test so may not answer accurately. Also factors such as upbringing, experiences, cultural factors, and trauma can affect one's EQ results.

IQ testing is limited in that they do not reveal the underlying cognitive processes of a person. Also because of the certain type of questions asked in this test, it can reflect bias against minorities or other cultures.

Why is EQ important?

We live in a world that involves interacting with many different types of people. We also live in a world that is changing and we may be thrown with surprises in our lives. By having a heightened ability to recognise, understand, and manage our emotions and those of others, this can allow people to live harmoniously with others in any situation. It also allows people to respond effectively to changes, stressful situations, and obstacles.

There are five components of Emotional Intelligence and I will explain each their importance:

1. Self-Awareness:

This is the ability to recognise what you're feeling, and understanding the effect of one's emotions and actions on other people. It is the understanding of their triggers and roots of their patterns of thinking and feeling. People who are self-aware have clarity in their beliefs, values and needs.

The importance of being self-aware is that it can help people recognise their strengths and limitations. By doing so, it can help cultivate one's growth and development by understanding the strengths to help them reach a goal but also understanding the weaknesses they have to overcome or grow in.

Also, by knowing ourselves internally, we can also know how to operate externally.

2. Self-Regulation:

It is being able to regulate and manage one's emotions and impulses. It is essentially thinking before acting and considering the consequences.

Feeling strong emotions is a very human experience. However, it is important to learn how to process those emotions and respond with appropriate behaviour because if we lack emotional regulation, it can lead to perpetuating the negative emotions which is a damaging consequence to oneself and our social relationships.

3. Motivation:

Motivation is being aware of what motivates them - why a person does something. It is what initiates, guides, and maintains goal-oriented behaviours. Emotionally intelligent people are motivated by things beyond money or fame.

Motivation is important because it is the driving force of human action. High-quality motivation can allow people to achieve goals. Without motivation, you cannot achieve anything.

Positive motivation can give you the desire to change life for the better, it enables you to prioritise things in your life in order to achieve the goal, it can help you overcome obstacles as you chase your goal, it can give you the correct attitude towards tasks no matter how hard it will be.

4. Empathy:

This is the ability to consider other people's feeling especially when making decisions. It is also understanding what other people are going through and being able to respond kindly and thoughtfully. Essentially it is putting yourself in another person's shoes.

In a professional lens, empathy is important because it allows you to better understand customers, colleagues, and help make beneficial decisions for the company.

In general life, empathy is important because it allows you understand others, and let others know that they heard. More likely, in return, they can empathise with you as well. This in return can strengthen the bonds that we interact with. As humans, we all have an inherent desire to be seen and heard.

5. Social Skills:

This is the ability to interact well with others - through the consideration of how you're feeling, what other's are feeling, and being an effective communicator.

Like the reasons provided for the above components, Social Skills are important to create and maintain relationships. Social connection allows us to feel a sense of belonging - by bonding our attitudes, values, and goals. A sense of belonging also comes with a sense of purpose, which can increase solidarity and fulfilment. Connection also allows us to have access to a support network of peers, can be a source of joy, expose us to a diversity of ideas, viewpoint, and personalities.
