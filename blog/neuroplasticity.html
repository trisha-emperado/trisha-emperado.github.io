Neuroplasticity

What is Neuroplasticity? Let’s break this word up…

Neuro refers to neurons, and neurons are the fundamental units of the brain and nervous system. Plasticity refers to malleability or ability to change. Hence neuroplasticity is the ability for the nervous system to change its activity by reorganising the structure, functions, or connections in response to intrinsic or extrinsic stimuli.

What is the importance of knowing this?

For a very long time, neuroscientists believed that our brains stopped developing in adolescence. They used to believe that a stroke, which caused significant motor function, was permanent. But with the advancement of our scientific understanding of brain development and injury, we are now aware that it is highly active and is malleable. Neurons that are frequently used create stronger neural connections.

That means our brains have the ability to:

  • Learn new things.
  • Enhance existing cognitive capabilities.
  • Recover from brain injury.
  • Strengthen areas where function was lost or declined.
  • Improve ‘mental fitness’.

How can you increase your neuroplasticity?

If you want to gain a new skill, there are five components to promote neuroplasticity.

  1. Challenge and Novelty

    The skill should be not too easy and new to you. There’s a sense of suffering but it’s okay - it only feels this way because it should be challenging, and it is new to you so you have never faced it before. Face the fears anyways.

  2. Intention

    Why do you want to learn this new skill? The new task must have meaning, relevance, importance to you. It’s important that it is personal to you because engaging in a new task needs to be associated with the feeling of being rewarded in order to promote neuroplastic change.

  3. Specific Attention

    Set a scheduled time to focus on this new skill you are learning. Practice makes perfect, right? Improvement in new skills are only executed with practice.

  4. Repetition and Intensity

    In order to create stronger neural connections, we must repeat tasks again and again. It is better to have shorter bouts of intense repetitio

  5. Time

    It takes time for changes to occur, so let’s be patient. As the natural rhythm of life consist of ebbs and flows, we need to be patient with ourselves when there are lapses. This is all part of normal life, so remember to refocus, adjust, and just keep swimming.

Growth Mindset

What is Growth mindset?

Before I explain what the ‘growth mindset’ is, I think explaining what the ‘fixed mindset’ is will paint a better picture of what the growth mindset is through comparison. A fixed mindset is when a person believes that their character, intelligence, and creativity is unchangeable. Also, it is when a person considers their success to be an affirmation of their inherent intelligence. They tend to perceive that maintaining a sense of being smart or being skilled is to avoid failure at all costs.

Contrastingly, the growth mindset embraces challenges and failures. Failure is not perceived as a sign of unsuccess or no intelligence, but as medium for growth. A person with a growth mindset views intelligence, abilities, and talents as something that can be learned and can be improved with effort and practice.

Having a growth mindset is important because it provides us with a healthy relationship to success and failure. People who are learning new skills will often face failure, so it is essential to have a growth mindset in order to persist despite failure. It will allow people to see failure as a path to success and just a stepping-stone in the learning process.
